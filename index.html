All Work

Brief
Conçu came in with an established identity and a grid we weren’t going to touch — the ask was to stay consistent, stay clean, and keep the momentum going without disrupting what was already working.
Vision
Every frame was built around letting the food speak — sharp, confident, and colour-forward. Backgrounds were chosen to complement rather than compete, keeping the aesthetic tight while quietly evolving the look over six months.
Brief
Two sibling brands, two distinct personalities — Rü needed to feel rooted and immersive, while Kin-Rü called for something more composed but with an edge.
Vision
For Rü, the frames were lived-in and layered — raw spices, loose ingredients, and subtle brand touches woven through menus, napkins, and the servers themselves. Kin-Rü stripped it back but dialled up the drama — clean compositions that let the plating and garnish do the storytelling.

Brief
A launch shoot with a blank slate — no grid, no precedent, just a stunning space and a full menu that needed to come to life from scratch.
Vision
Natural light was the hero from the start — the café’s open layout made it a no-brainer to shoot with whatever the sky gave us. Come evening, the moonlit ambiance shaped everything, and the chefs and owners were brought into the creative process to make each frame feel like it truly belonged to the place.
Brief
Tai Tai’s identity is built on handcrafted desserts made with real, natural ingredients — bold, playful, and unapologetically premium. The ask was to let the content reflect exactly that, without dressing it up beyond what it already was.
Vision
We pulled directly from what the brand had already built — the warm yellows, the crisp whites, the energy of the space — and carried that colour palette into every frame. Foregrounds and backgrounds were dressed with the raw toppings and natural ingredients that actually go into the desserts, so what you see in the photo is exactly what goes into your hands.

Storytellers For Food & Drink.

A dish carries the weight of its ingredients, the hands that made it, the hours behind it — and the culture it came from. Our job is to make sure none of that gets lost in the frame.

Ember & Frames is a visual content studio and social media partner built for the food and beverage world. From restaurant interiors and bar programmes to hotel dining and editorial spreads, we create photography and film that feels as alive on screen as it does in the room — warm, considered, and impossible to scroll past.
